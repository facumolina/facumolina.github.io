\section{Employment History}

\begin{longtable}{>{\raggedleft\arraybackslash}p{0.10\textwidth} p{0.86\textwidth}}

2025/2026 & \textbf{Assistant Professor} - \textit{Complutense University of Madrid}, Madrid, Spain. \\  
& \textbf{Research}. As an Assistant Professor, I conduct research on software testing and analysis and AI4SE. Currently, I'm the leading research in the following projects: \\ & \\
& - \textit{DoGFuzz:} documentation-guided fuzzing for testing Python libraries. This undergoing project involves the use of Large Language Models (LLMs) -- e.g., \href{https://mistral.ai/news/mistral-large}{Mistral Large}, \href{https://openai.com/index/gpt-4/}{OpenAI's GPT-4} -- to extract input constraints from API's documentation, and then use such constraints to guide a fuzzing process for testing APIs in Python libraries, including 
deep learning libraries
like \href{https://www.tensorflow.org/}{TensorFlow} and \href{https://pytorch.org/}{PyTorch}. \\ & \\
& - \textit{Metamorphic testing for AI models:} this undergoing project aims at developing techniques for the automated inference of metamorphic relations to test the robustness of AI models with the goal of improving the reliability of AI-based software. \\ & \\
& Additionally, I collaborate in the following research project: \\ & \\
& - \textit{ASCEND:} industry project coordinated by Boeing Aerospace Spain SLU and ANZEN Aerospace Engineering SL, where I work as a member of a team from the IMDEA Software Institute, my previous institution, implementing formal verification-based techniques for model-based safety analyses. \\ & \\
& \textbf{Teaching}. Currently, I have teaching duties in the following courses: \\ & \\
& - \textit{Programming II} (Programación II): I am responsible of a first-year course focused on Python programming for a Data Science degree. \href{https://estudiosestadisticos.ucm.es/estudios/grado-cienciadelosdatosaplicada-plan-806299}{[program]} \\ & \\
& - \textit{Programming Technology II} (Tecnología de Programación II): I assist in the practical classes of
a second-year programming-intensive course for Computer Science degrees. \href{https://informatica.ucm.es/estudios/grado-adeinginformatica-plan-901981}{[program]} \\ & \\

2022/2025 & \textbf{Postdoctoral Researcher} - \textit{IMDEA Software Institute}, Madrid, Spain. \\
& I worked as a postdoctoral researcher on software testing and analysis, which included developing and implementing novel program analysis techniques, and disseminating our research on top tier software engineering venues. During this period, I was the leading researcher in the following projects: \\ & \\
& - \textit{Deep Learning Library Testing:} study of the use of machine learning (ML) classifiers to improve the effectiveness of the testing of deep learning (DL) libraries. ML classiers predicts input validity, a key aspect in the testing of DL libraries, as they often have complex input constraints. We implemented a \href{https://sites.google.com/view/ml4dllconstraints}{prototype} for testing \href{https://www.tensorflow.org/}{TensorFlow} and \href{https://pytorch.org/}{PyTorch} \\ & \\

& - \textit{State Field Coverage:} we propose a novel metric to assess the quality of test oracles in Java by analyzing the proportion of object states, as statically defined in the corresponding classes, that may be accessed during test execution. A static analysis-based \href{https://zenodo.org/records/17255287}{prototype} is implemented to analyze popular Java libraries, e.g., \href{https://commons.apache.org/}{Apache Commons}. \\ & \\
& - \textit{FixCheck:} patch correctness assessment improvement based on random testing and Large Language Models (LLMs). The goal was to automatically provide tests revealing the incorrectness of a patch. FixCheck, a tool for analyzing Java patches is implemented using Java, Python, Docker and also the \href{https://huggingface.co/}{Hugging Face} and \href{https://ollama.com/}{Ollama} frameworks to support LLMs. FixCheck is available at: \href{https://github.com/facumolina/fixcheck}{https://github.com/facumolina/fixcheck} \\ & \\

& Additionally, I collaborated in the following research projects: \\ & \\
& - \textit{Express:} automated generation of test oracles in the form of class invariants using a simulated annealing evolutionary algorithm. The goal was to infer precise invariants to be used as part of a regression testing process. A \href{https://github.com/JuanmaCopia/express}{prototype} for generating class invariants for Java classes is implemented. \\ & \\
& \textit{MemoRIA:} automated inference of metamorphic oracles combining grammar-based fuzzing, dynamic analysis and SAT-based analysis. A \href{https://zenodo.org/records/10683011}{prototype} is implemented in Java, using \href{https://randoop.github.io/randoop/}{Randoop} (for dynamic analysis) and \href{https://alloytools.org/}{Alloy} (for SAT-based analysis). \\ & \\
& \textit{PLI:} symbolic execution for programs that manipulate complex heap-allocated data. A \href{https://github.com/JuanmaCopia/spf-pli}{prototype} is implemented in Java on top of the \href{https://github.com/SymbolicPathFinder/jpf-symbc}{Symbolic PathFinder} engine.
\\ & \\

2017/2022 & \textbf{Ph.D. Student} - \textit{FaMaF, University of Córdoba}, Argentina. \\ 
& My PhD was focused on developing techniques for the \textit{automated generation of test oracles} using search-based and learning-based techniques (evolutionary algorithms and neural nets). My advisor was Prof. Nazareno Aguirre and my dissertation is available in the UNC \href{https://rdu.unc.edu.ar/handle/11086/26692}{digital repository}. The main contributions of my thesis are: \\ & \\
& - \textit{SpecFuzzer:} automated inference of test oracles in the form of class specifications using grammar-based fuzzing. SpecFuzzer, a \href{https://github.com/facumolina/specfuzzer}{tool} for inferring oracles for Java classes is implemented using Java and Python, and built on top of the \href{https://plse.cs.washington.edu/daikon/}{Daikon} dynamic invariant detector. SpecFuzzer is available at: \href{https://github.com/facumolina/specfuzzer}{https://github.com/facumolina/specfuzzer}. \\ & \\
& - \textit{EvoSpex:} search-based inference of postconditions of Java methods using genetic algorithms. The EvoSpex tool, implemented in Java using the \href{https://homepages.ecs.vuw.ac.nz/~lensenandr/jgap/documentation/}{JGAP} library, aims to produce a specification of the method's current behavior, in the form of postcondition assertions. EvoSpex is available at: \href{https://github.com/facumolina/evospex}{https://github.com/facumolina/evospex}. \\ & \\
& - \textit{NNInvs:} data structure object classification using artificial neural networks. A first \href{https://sites.google.com/site/learninginvariants}{prototype} is implemented using Java and Python, relying on the \href{https://scikit-learn.org/stable/index.html}{scikit-learn} library. A second \href{https://sites.google.com/site/learninginvariants}{prototype} is developed (to be used in the context of symbolic execution) using the \href{https://keras.io/}{Keras} deep learning library. \\ & \\
